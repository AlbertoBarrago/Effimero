How it works

Every visitor is identified by a hash that rotates out of existence every day:

At midnight UTC the salt is replaced with a fresh random value and the old one is deleted. From that moment, yesterday's hashes are mathematically uncorrelatable with today's: there is nothing to subpoena, leak, or de-anonymize. Cross-day tracking isn't forbidden; it's impossible.

What you still get

Daily unique visitors

Accurate per-day uniques via HyperLogLog. The hash itself is never stored, only a cardinality sketch.

Live visitors

Who's on your site right now, from a 5-minute rolling window.

Pages & referrers

Top content and traffic sources, with referrers reduced to hostname only.

Tech & geo

Browser, OS, device class, language, and country, stored only as aggregate counters.

What you give up, on purpose

  • No returning-visitor metrics. The daily salt rotation makes cross-day identity impossible by design.
  • No multi-touch attribution. No persistent identity means no journey stitching.
  • Shared networks blur. Same IP + same browser version within one day counts as one visitor.
  • Counts are probabilistic. HyperLogLog carries a ~0.81% standard error.

These are honest trade-offs, not bugs. If you need cross-day funnels, Effimero is the wrong tool, and it will never quietly become one.

Quick start

Self-host with Docker in one command:

git clone https://github.com/albertobarrago/effimero && cd effimero
docker compose up -d

Then drop the snippet on your site:

<script src="https://your-host/effimero.js" data-site="my-site" defer></script>

Open the cockpit dashboard at https://your-host/. That's it: no consent banner needed, because nothing identifying is ever stored, on the device or the server.

Engineering principles

Privacy by construction

Guarantees come from the algorithm, not the privacy policy. If the operator turned malicious, the data still wouldn't exist.

Separation of concerns

Hashing, enrichment, storage, and transport are isolated modules behind small interfaces. Swap Redis or the UA parser without touching ingest logic.

Battle-tested dependencies

Proven libraries (Fastify, ioredis, ua-parser-js) where they earn their keep; no wheel reinvention, no dependency bloat either.

Honest limits

Every trade-off is documented up front. An analytics tool that overstates its accuracy is lying to you.

Free & open source. Really.

There is no cloud plan, no premium tier, no waitlist. Effimero is licensed under the AGPL-3.0: you self-host it with one Docker command, own your data end to end, and the license guarantees it stays free. Anyone can offer Effimero hosting to others, as long as their changes stay open too.
